Context
Wonsulting provides consulting and coaching to help those from non-target schools and non-traditional backgrounds land their dream careers.
Problem Discovery
When Googling "Non target school, job search", I'm faced with endless numbers of forums expressing feelings of discouragement, concern, and confusion regarding the job search. On Youtube, there is an abundance of videos showcasing success stories of those from non target schools and their "secret" as to how they managed to land their dream job. It was evident that there is a need for a solution in this problem space.
Using our personal experience as well as testimonies from previous Wonsulting clients, the team began to map out the job search journey. We asked ourselves the question:
Job seekers go through and apply to tens, maybe hundreds of job postings during one round of their job search. We can't necessarily say the same thing with interviews and hiring contracts. It was evident that the "Prep" and "Apply" stages of the job search journey yielded the most opportunity for impact.

My research identified a clear area of focus - resume creation.
Overview
Solution
ResumeAI - an AI powered resume builder that generates personalized and impactful resume points.

Impact
100K user sign ups within 3 months of launch.

User Research
To better understand how job seekers currently create their resumes and their pain points, LinkedIn polls and user interviews were conducted.
Design Principles
User research not only uncovered key insights regarding their job search journey, but acted as the rationale for our future solution's design principles.
Designing V1
The initial version of our solution was a resume bullet point generator heavily inspired by the STAR method. Not only did I talk to job seekers, but hiring managers and recruiters. When asked "What makes a good resume?", almost all responses included something that the STAR method consisted of, or directly referred to the STAR method itself.

The initial solution was simple. Users answer 5 questions, select past or present tense, and generate bullet points. From there, they can edit, copy, or regenerate points.
Testing V1
I conducted usability tests with four job seekers to understand their mental models when approaching this tool. The usability test was a great method to validate my assumptions.

Key Insight
3 out of 4 job seekers felt that the process to generate a bullet point is too time consuming and extensive. One interviewee mentioned that they wouldn't use the tool because they don't need to create a bullet point completely from scratch. They expressed that they have a good idea of what they want their bullet point to look like, but they would want to improve upon it.
Testing made me realize that there are a variety of users that will be interacting with my product. Beginners need a lot more handholding and may not mind the extensive STAR method approach. Intermediate and Advanced users however, require less guidance, and want a quick and easy way to revise and improve upon their resume. My current design only considers the Beginners.
Designing V2
After receiving that feedback during testing, my goal for the next iteration was to create a product that offers different experiences for Beginner, Intermediate, and Advanced resume writers. In V2, users can choose if they want to create a resume bullet point from scratch using the 5 question STAR method, or they can simply have our AI rewrite and improve an existing bullet point.
Switching States
I explored a couple different ways to convey the switch between the "Create New Points" and "Improve Points". Ultimately I chose the "Tab to Switch" design as it depicted the clearest change in state. My only concern was that the lack of component (ie: button, toggle switch) makes it unclear if the tab is clickable, however, the change in colour when hovering over the text was able to solve this concern.
Key Insight
After completing my V2 designs, I presented them to my team to receive feedback.
This insight pushed towards a different look and feel for our product - a product that doesn't just generate the content for a resume bullet point, but one that helps users create an entire resume from scratch.
Designing V3
There are many other resume builders and templates in the market however, what made ResumeAI special is the resume bullet point generation that will be embedded within the resume builder. Because of this unique feature, it was crucial to find an optimal layout that makes the embedded AI generator seamlessly fit in the product.
First Iteration
I began my designs with the side by side layout as it seemed to be the most common in resume builder competitors. This would allow users to seamlessly transition to our product from theirs.
Second Iteration
Due to the disadvantages discovered in the first iteration, I created a new iteration using the "Word Document Style", where users can simply click on the document and edit right then and there - similar to a Microsoft Word or Google Doc. This reduced the added work required for users to input their changes and open/collapse sections.
Third Iteration
With the second iteration, I began to ask myself, "What makes this better than a Word document?" Users could essentially copy and paste the resume template onto a Word Document and potentially have an even better experience, where they can modify margins, select from a slew of fonts, etc. And although this design enables more flexibility for the user, I realized that it offered too much flexibility. ResumeAI needed to cater to those building resumes and offer functionalities that Microsoft Word couldn't.

My third iteration implemented small widgets that handle different types of data inputs. I had date pickers, location mapping, dropdowns, among other components, in order to make resume builder experience easier than just typing it out on a document.
Final Designs
Manage Resumes
Successful resumes are those that are tailored and customized to each posting. We encourage creating multiple resume versions depending on employer, roles, locations, etc. Users can organize and manage their files here.
Onboarding
A walkthrough tour that spotlights key features to familiarize users and ensure they are getting the most out of the product.
Edit & Export
Mimics familiar document editing experiences (Google Docs, Adobe Reader, Word) but prioritize key features for simplicity.
Generate Bullet Points
Our initial bullet point generator embedded within the resume builder.
Payment - Buying Tokens
The payment flow prioritizes transparency throughout the entire payment journey to reassure safety and security in payment.
Handoff & Launch
The resume builder has been handed off and is currently live at https://resumai.wonsulting.com/ ! Feel free to try it for yourself.
Measuring Success
Quantitative data is a great indicator of success. The product managers and I tracked key performance indicators (Daily active users, activity per session, copy rate, export rate etc.) to determine the success of ResumAI.

Currently, ResumAI has an action rate of 95%, where 95% of users either copy/use a generated bullet point, or export a resume per session.
Next Steps
This project is still a work in progress. ResumAI will soon grow into a product that can empower job seekers throughout the entire job search journey - but for now, there are still many other ideas regarding how I hope to improve the resume building experience.
Reflection
This project had a lot of versions and iterations. This meant a lot of back and forth with the engineers. I learned that frequent and constant communication and good design documentation really streamlines the handoff process.

I also learned the importance of taking the time to brainstorm to ensure that all avenues are being explored.
